# Episode 4 – Scenes

## Scene: "The HOA Meeting" (Eldritch Portal Deadpan)

**Duration**: ~1:15 seconds  
**Location**: Suburban community center meeting room / parking lot at dusk  
**Time of Year**: Late October, the night after Halloween  
**Tone**: Bureaucratic banality meets Lovecraftian cosmic horror  
**New Cinematic Joke Element**: The HOA president maintains Robert's Rules of Order while the apocalypse unfolds outside the window  
**Music**: Dull Muzak version of "Take Me Home, Country Roads" plays inside; a low, swelling theremin drone creeps in from outside at ~0:35

---

### 0:00 – 0:10  "Call to Disorder"

**Description**  
Seven residents sit in metal folding chairs in a fluorescent-lit community center room. A whiteboard behind them reads "AGENDA: 1. Recycling bins 2. Pool hours 3. Fence heights." HOA president DALE (mid-60s, short-sleeve button-down, clipboard) taps a gavel with zero authority.

**Camera**  
Wide establishing shot, slightly low angle making the room feel even more oppressive.

**Dialog**  
DALE: Item one. Karen submitted a complaint that Brad's recycling bin is — and I quote — "the wrong shade of democratic blue."

KAREN (crossing arms): It looks like anarchy blue, Dale.

BRAD (slumped): It's the bin the city gave me, Karen.

**Grok Imagine Prompt**  
"Wide shot of drab suburban community center meeting room with seven tired residents in folding chairs, fluorescent lighting, whiteboard with HOA agenda, bland beige walls, deadpan comedy atmosphere, filmic, Grok Imagine style"

---

### 0:10 – 0:20  "The First Signs"

**Description**  
Through the large window behind Dale, a thin purple crack of light appears in the parking lot sky. No one notices. Dale adjusts his glasses and moves to item two.

**Camera**  
Push-in on Dale, with the window clearly visible behind him. Rack focus: Dale in foreground, purple light growing in background.

**Dialog**  
DALE: Item two. The Johnson kid threw a pool party that went past the posted 9 PM curfew.

MR. JOHNSON (defensive): It was 9:03, Dale. Three minutes. My son has anxiety.

DALE (writing): Anxiety doesn't excuse municipal code, Gary.

**Sound Design**  
- Distant low rumble, barely perceptible under the fluorescent hum
- A single dog barks outside, then stops abruptly

**Grok Imagine Prompt**  
"Medium shot of frustrated HOA president at community center meeting, purple eldritch light beginning to crack open in sky visible through window behind him, no one notices, mundane horror comedy, filmic, Grok Imagine style"

---

### 0:20 – 0:35  "Tentacle Violations"

**Description**  
The sky tears open. Three massive bioluminescent tentacles descend into the parking lot, crushing a minivan. Inside, Dale is now on item three. A resident named PATRICIA points at the window.

**Camera**  
Quick cut to the window: tentacles flailing, minivan alarm blaring. Cut back to Dale, who doesn't turn around.

**Dialog**  
PATRICIA (raising hand): Dale? There's something outside that I believe violates section 4-B of the landscaping covenant.

DALE (not looking up): Is it over six feet tall?

PATRICIA (squinting): It's... difficult to measure. It's waving.

DALE: Anything over six feet requires a variance and a notarized architect's drawing. Take it up with zoning.

**Sound Design**  
- Minivan alarm muffled through glass
- Deep wet THWAP as tentacle smacks pavement
- Inside: someone coughs, Muzak continues

**Grok Imagine Prompt**  
"Chaotic split-composition shot: inside boring HOA meeting room residents debating, outside window massive glowing tentacles destroying cars in parking lot, comedic contrast, cinematic lighting, Grok Imagine style"

---

### 0:35 – 0:50  "Robert's Rules of Armageddon"

**Description**  
The room trembles. Dust falls from ceiling tiles. A tentacle presses against the window, revealing an eye the size of a dinner plate. Dale checks his watch.

**Camera**  
Slow push-in on Dale. Tentacle eye dominates the window behind him, blinking.

**Dialog**  
DALE: We're running long. Motion to extend the meeting by fifteen minutes.

BRAD: Seconded.

DALE: All in favor?

(Everyone raises a hand. Dale counts.)

DALE: Opposed?

PATRICIA (staring at tentacle): I just think we should at least acknowledge —

DALE (sharp): Patricia. Robert's Rules. We have seventeen open items.

**Sound Design**  
- Theremin drone now audible, weaving with Muzak
- Window glass creaking under tentacle pressure
- Dale's pen scratching paper

**Grok Imagine Prompt**  
"Close-up of deadpan HOA president in community center meeting, giant eldritch eyeball pressed against window directly behind him, dust falling from ceiling, bureaucratic horror comedy, high detail, filmic, Grok Imagine style"

---

### 0:50 – 1:05  "The Pull-Back Begins"

**Description**  
Camera begins a smooth backward dolly through the window, passing the tentacle. The entire neighborhood is now visible: houses being gently lifted by tendrils, cars floating skyward, a streetlamp bending like a wilted flower. Inside the meeting room, tiny figures still sit in their folding chairs.

**Camera**  
Continuous backward move through the glass, transitioning from interior to exterior. Keep the room visible as a lit box inside the chaos.

**Dialog**  
DALE (tiny, muffled, from inside): Item twelve. Linda's decorative gargoyle.

LINDA (O.S., tiny): It's vintage!

DALE (O.S.): It's a covenant violation, Linda. Vintage gargoyles still count.

**Sound Design**  
- Muzak fades
- Theremin drone swells, joined by low choral voices
- Wind rising

**Grok Imagine Prompt**  
"Smooth backward dolly shot moving out of community center window into parking lot, HOA meeting visible as tiny lit room inside massive Lovecraftian neighborhood destruction, tentacles lifting houses, cars floating, cinematic scale, Grok Imagine style"

---

### 1:05 – 1:15+  "The Sky Holds the Agenda"

**Description**  
Final wide shot. The neighborhood is a diorama of floating debris and writhing appendages. The community center remains intact, its fluorescent lights still buzzing. In the center of the torn sky: not a monster's face, but an impossibly vast clipboard.

**Camera**  
Slow upward tilt to the sky, hold for three beats.

**Dialog**  
DALE (V.O., distant, echoing): Motion to adjourn denied. We still have to vote on the pool heater.

**Sound Design**  
- Single piano chord, unresolved
- Choral drone fades
- Final sound: a gavel, tiny and crisp

**Grok Imagine Prompt**  
"Epic final wide shot of suburban neighborhood being consumed by eldritch void, tiny community center still lit and intact at center, massive cosmic clipboard visible in torn sky above, mysterious bureaucratic apocalypse, comedic cosmic horror, filmic, Grok Imagine style"

---

## Production Notes

- The **HOA president** is the comedic anchor — his refusal to acknowledge the apocalypse makes every tentacle funnier by contrast.
- Total runtime: ~1:15
- Best generated in 3 clips: interior meeting, tentacle arrival, pull-back + sky reveal
- The Muzak-to-theremin audio transition is key to the joke — keep the inside world sonically dead while chaos grows outside
- The cosmic clipboard reveal pays off the bureaucratic theme: even the universe has an HOA
