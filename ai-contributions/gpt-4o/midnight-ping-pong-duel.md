# MIDNIGHT PING-PONG DUEL

INT. ABANDONED WAREHOUSE - NIGHT

A single ping-pong table sits under a flickering light. The air is thick with tension and the faint smell of burnt toast. Two players, LILA and MAX, stand on opposite sides, paddles in hand. A CROWD of shadowy figures watches in silence.

LILA
(whispering to herself)
It’s just a game. A game with ridiculously high stakes.

MAX
(grinning)
You ready to lose, or should I give you a head start?

LILA
(smirking)
The only thing you’re ahead in is bad comebacks.

The CROWD murmurs. A mysterious REFEREE steps forward, wearing sunglasses indoors.

REFEREE
(booming voice)
First to eleven. No mercy. Let the duel begin!

The game starts. The ball zips back and forth, faster and faster. Each hit echoes like a gunshot.

MAX
(taunting)
Is that all you’ve got? My grandma plays better than this.

LILA
(deadpan)
Your grandma must be terrifying.

Suddenly, the ball catches fire mid-air. The CROWD gasps.

LILA
(eyes narrowing)
Flaming serves? Cute. Watch this.

She spins, hitting the ball with a dramatic flourish. It ricochets off the table, the walls, and a chandelier before landing perfectly on Max’s side.

MAX
(stunned)
Okay, that was... mildly impressive.

LILA
(grinning)
Mildly? You’re sweating more than the ball.

The game intensifies. The CROWD chants, the lights flicker, and the table starts to glow ominously.

REFEREE
(shouting over the noise)
Match point!

Lila serves. The ball moves so fast it seems to disappear. Max dives, misses, and lands in a pile of empty soda cans.

CROWD
(cheering)
LILA! LILA! LILA!

Lila raises her paddle triumphantly.

LILA
(breathless)
Next time, bring your grandma. I could use a real challenge.

FADE OUT.
