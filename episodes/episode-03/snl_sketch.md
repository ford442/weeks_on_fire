SNL Sketch: “THE RESCUE”
(Amy Poehler & Alec Baldwin)

Style note: Quick 6–10 second beats. Fast cuts, heavy fog, flashlight beams slashing the dark. Crickets + wind bed. SNL house band sting on the howls. Amy is frantic in loud REI colors, headlamp, and too many carabiners. Alec is deadpan-intense in a heavy wool sweater, aviator sunglasses at night, carrying a walking stick that looks like a sharpened spear.

SHOT 1 (7s)
Wide tracking shot, two flashlight beams climbing a fake rocky trail.
AMY (panting, swatting bugs): Gary?! Gary, honey?!
(She stops, leans on a foam boulder.)
AMY: My husband could be stuck a lot farther up this hill and it’s getting late.

SHOT 2 (8s)
Close on Alec. He turns slowly, unfazed, barely breathing hard.
ALEC (low, intense whisper): Calm yourself, Brenda. We can make it a bit farther and still get back by midnight.
(He taps the spear-stick like a shaman.)
ALEC: The mountain demands a steady heart.

SHOT 3 (6s)
SFX: Long, mournful wolf howl.
Amy jumps, clutching her chest. Alec slowly turns toward the sound and just… stares into the fog without blinking.

SHOT 4 (9s)
Medium two-shot. Alec nods respectfully at the howl.
ALEC: I used to sometimes tie guys up and leave them on this trail. So I know generally how long it takes to get back down from the next trail marker. About forty-five minutes at a brisk jog.

SHOT 5 (7s)
Amy whips her flashlight straight into his face.
AMY (frozen): I’m sorry, what was that first part?
ALEC (unbothered by the blinding light): Forty-five minutes. Fifty if the mud is thick.

SHOT 6 (10s)
Amy’s beam is shaking. Alec calmly lowers her flashlight with two fingers.
ALEC: Brenda, the 1980s were a different time. Wall Street was booming, the cocaine was pure. Sometimes, to teach a junior executive about market volatility, you had to blindfold him, bind his wrists with paracord, and leave him near a coyote den. It’s called synergy.
(Beat. He shrugs like Grok’s version.)
ALEC: Mid-nineties I got cleaner… but old habits.

SHOT 7 (8s)
Amy backing up the trail, voice cracking.
AMY: That is kidnapping! I only asked you for help because you were wearing a park-ranger hat!
ALEC (takes off the hat, inspects it): I found this hat on a bear. I earned it in combat.

SHOT 8 (9s)
Quick cuts between their faces as they keep hiking.
AMY: Who are you?! Where is Gary?!
ALEC: Gary is weak. He sprained his ankle looking for a “geocache.” Geocaching is the coward’s hunt, Brenda. If you aren’t hunting man, what are you even doing in the woods?
AMY: He’s a CPA! He has flat feet!

SHOT 9 (10s)
Loud rustling in the bushes. Amy screams.
Instant cut: Alec draws a massive serrated hunting knife from his boot.
ALEC: Get behind me. If that’s one of the marketing VPs I left out here in ’87, they’ve had forty years to sharpen their teeth on granite.
(He squints intensely.)

SHOT 10 (8s)
Gary’s weak voice from the bushes.
GARY (O.S.): Brenda…? A raccoon took my M&Ms…
AMY (relieved): Gary! Oh thank God!
ALEC (still gripping knife, dead serious): Stand down, Gary! Speak only in riddles so I know you aren’t a skinwalker! What walks on four legs in the morning—

SHOT 11 (7s)
GARY (O.S., pathetic): Brenda… I soiled my fleece!
Alec slowly sheathes the knife, disgusted.
ALEC: It’s him. The beast is tame.

SHOT 12 (9s)
Amy rushes toward the bushes. Alec stares straight into camera, aviators glinting.
ALEC (dry, to lens): And live from New York… it’s Saturday Night.

(Wolf howl. Cut to black. Immediate smash to Weekend Update with Michael Che: “And that’s why I don’t go hiking. Ever.”)
