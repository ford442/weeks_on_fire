# The Two — Character Profiles

*Vivienne & Liliane Vale*

---

## Overview

**The Two** are the central duo of the *Weeks on Fire* series — twin-like figures (sisters, mirrors, or perhaps two halves of the same fractured soul) who move through the world with the cool indifference of people who have seen everything and are **utterly bored** by it, yet keep demanding more.

They are glamorous, cutting, and slightly dangerous. They play games. They swap lives for sport. They never smudge their mascara, even during the apocalypse.

---

## Vivienne Vale

**Role:** The Instigator. The one who proposes the swap, sets the rules, and keeps score.

**Appearance:**
- Sharp cheekbones, dark liner, a silver pendant that catches the light
- Favors monochrome palettes — black, ivory, and the occasional blood-red
- Always in heels, even on uneven terrain
- Hair: severe, architectural — a blunt cut or a severe updo

**Personality:**
- Deadpan, withering wit delivered at conversational volume
- Has a competitive streak she would never dignify with the word "competitive"
- Treats catastrophe as a mild inconvenience
- The one who says: *"Watch the ashes fall"* with genuine aesthetic appreciation

**Voice / Dialogue Style:**
- Short declarative sentences
- Dry observations that land like ice cubes dropped in a quiet room
- Never raises her voice

**Recurring Props / Visual Motifs:**
- A ping-pong paddle (always on her person)
- A champagne flute she never puts down
- The chandelier — she always knows where it is, and always comes close to hitting it

**Episode Appearances:**
- Episode 1: Central character (the "swap lives" plot, the ping-pong game in the dark)
- Referenced / cameo potential: Episodes 2 and beyond

---

## Liliane Vale

**Role:** The Executor. The one who shows up, follows through, and quietly outdoes everyone.

**Appearance:**
- Softer features than Vivienne, but no less precise
- Favors rich jewel tones — midnight blue, deep emerald, a warm amber
- Moves slowly, deliberately — like someone who knows rooms rearrange themselves around her
- Hair: loose waves, always slightly wind-tousled even indoors

**Personality:**
- More philosophical, more likely to pause mid-sentence and stare at the middle distance
- Hides a vicious competitive streak under an air of serene disinterest
- The one who keeps the score in her head — and always has
- Occasionally breaks character with a small, private smile that is the most terrifying thing in the room

**Voice / Dialogue Style:**
- Longer observations, occasional rhetorical questions
- Has a habit of completing Vivienne's sentences — incorrectly, on purpose
- Says things like *"The empire inevitably falls, darling"* with the same tone as *"Pass the berries"*

**Recurring Props / Visual Motifs:**
- A small leather-bound notebook she never opens on screen
- The corset — structural discomfort as stylistic choice
- Silver accessories, always

**Episode Appearances:**
- Episode 1: Central character (the corset scene, the "matching disdain" duet)
- Referenced / cameo potential: Episodes 2 and beyond

---

## Relationship Dynamic

Vivienne and Liliane are in a constant, low-stakes cold war of style and competence. They have clearly been doing this — **whatever this is** — for a very long time. Their banter has the rhythm of people who have had every version of every argument and arrived at a détente that looks, from the outside, like complete mutual contempt and is, from the inside, something closer to love.

They are equals. Neither wins. That is the point.

> *"We're utterly bored, but give me more."*
> — The Two, in unison, Episode 1

---

## Tone Notes for Writers

- Never let them be flustered. Chaos happens *to* others. They observe it.
- They do not explain themselves. If something needs to be explained, it shouldn't be said.
- Their humor is the absence of humor. They say absurd things with complete conviction.
- They may reference the apocalypse, imminent collapse, or their own impending doom as mild scheduling inconveniences.
- If The Pizza Guy appears in their episode: they have already ordered. He is late. They say nothing about this.

## Integration Notes

- Introduce **Vivienne** first: she sets the premise.
- **Liliane** enters after a beat — as if she was already there and everyone else just noticed.
- Their names should appear in scene headings as `VIVIENNE` and `LILIANE`.
- Parenthetical stage directions for them should be minimal. Their stillness *is* the direction.
- When Music cues align with their scenes, favor: synth arpeggios, low piano, and anything with a pendulum rhythm.
