# "NEON APOTHEOSIS" — Musical Cutaway Scene

*A screenplay interlude for a moments of comedic chaos and romantic absurdism set in a collapsing dance venue.*

---

## INT. THE MERIDIAN — A WAREHOUSE RAVE — NIGHT

*Strobe lights blur the walls. The DJ booth is on fire (literally). Dancers move in slow-motion through the haze. A MAN in a sequined coat and a WOMAN in a velvet mask stand in the center of the floor, perfectly still—an island of calm amid the chaos.*

**WOMAN**  
(adjusting her mask)  
Is this the part where we fall in love, or the part where the building collapses?

**MAN**  
(dead serious)  
Both. They're simultaneous.

**WOMAN**  
Of course they are.

*She steps closer. He extends his hand. Thunder rumbles. The ceiling cracks.*

**MAN**  
The DJ's laptop is melting.

**WOMAN**  
(glancing back)  
That's the third one this month.

**MAN**  
And yet... the bass line is *perfect*.

*The synth swells. They begin to dance—not gracefully, but with absolute commitment. Around them, other dancers move like ghosts, like they've been here for years, like they'll be here forever.*

---

## CHORUS (20 seconds)

**WOMAN & MAN** *(singing in unison)*

*We came here looking for a moment,*  
*Found a kingdom made of smoke,*  
*Where the DJ plays the funeral march*  
*And we all dance through the joke.*

*The floor is melting.*  
*The walls are breathing.*  
*But darling, your hand fits mine,*  
*So let's waltz through the apocalypse*  
*One more time.*

---

## INT. THE MERIDIAN — CONTINUOUS

*A BOUNCER, impossibly tall and serene, stands by the exit holding a clipboard. He checks off names on a list.*

**BOUNCER**  
(to the fleeing crowd)  
Name, please.

**FLEEING PERSON #1**  
The building is on fire!

**BOUNCER**  
Name. *Please*.

*The crowd shuffles through, calling out names. The BOUNCER checks each one off with a pen that writes in gold ink. He is unmoved by the chaos.*

---

## BACK ON THE DANCE FLOOR

*The WOMAN pulls away slightly. The MAN's eyes are closed, still dancing.*

**WOMAN**  
(shouting over the music)  
How did we even get here?

**MAN**  
(eyes still closed)  
I took the 11:47 bus. It made an unscheduled stop in hell.

**WOMAN**  
That explains the velvet seats.

**MAN**  
I thought those were your eyelashes.

*She laughs—a real, surprised laugh. The ceiling shakes.*

---

## BREAKDOWN (15 seconds)

*The music drops to a single synthesizer note—sustained, haunting, beautiful.*

**WOMAN** *(singing, alone)*

*If the world is ending,*  
*Let it end like this:*  
*With sequins and synthesizers,*  
*Your hand in mine,*  
*And no idea what comes next.*

*The synth swells back in. All the dancers turn in unison, facing toward the center—toward the WOMAN and MAN. For a moment, they are all breathing together, as one.*

---

## CLIMAX (20 seconds)

**EVERYONE** *(singing)*

*The wheel is turning,*  
*The fire is burning,*  
*The apocalypse tastes like champagne,*  
*And we're all here together*  
*In the moment before the end,*  
*And it's beautiful.*  
*It's beautiful.*  
*It's beautiful.*

*The building doesn't collapse. Instead, it transforms—the walls become mirrors, reflecting infinite versions of the dancers, the WOMAN and MAN at the center of each reflection, always dancing, always together.*

---

## FINAL BEAT (5 seconds)

*The DJ (unburnt, impossibly) hits one last chord. The lights cut to black.*

*In the darkness, a single spotlight finds the WOMAN and MAN, now standing still, foreheads touching.*

**WOMAN**  
(whispered)  
Same time next week?

**MAN**  
(whispered back)  
The building will be rebuilt by then. We could dance in something new.

**WOMAN**  
(a smile in her voice)  
No. This place. This burning, ridiculous place.

**MAN**  
Then it'll have to be rebuilt by morning.

*Blackout.*

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Musical Style:** Euphoric trance/hardstyle buildup → ambient break → climactic drop (following Minimax Music 90s dance aesthetic)

**Visual Direction:** Strobe-and-smoke practical effects; mirror work in climax for infinite reflection motif (AI-generated mirror landscapes via Grok Imagine)

**Tone:** Darkly comedic romance set against existential collapse; treats apocalypse as a familiar, almost boring backdrop to human connection

**Runtime:** ~2:40 (fits typical musical cutaway length)

**Character Arcs:** Both characters begin detached ("Is this love or collapse?") and end committed ("Let's do this impossible thing again tomorrow")
