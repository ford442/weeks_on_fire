# LATE NIGHT LAUNDRY — SCREENPLAY CUTAWAY

INT. LAUNDROMAT — MIDNIGHT

A row of ancient washing machines rumbles like distant thunder. Fluorescent lights flicker. Two CHARACTERS, JORDAN and CASEY, sit on plastic chairs, surrounded by baskets of mismatched socks.

JORDAN
(staring at a spinning machine)
If this thing goes any faster, we’ll hit time travel before rinse cycle.

CASEY
(grinning)
I just want my hoodie back before it evolves into a sentient towel.

A DRYER BUZZES. A sock launches out like a cannonball, landing on Jordan’s head.

JORDAN
(deadpan, sock still on head)
I think it’s trying to communicate. If it asks for detergent, run.

CASEY
(leaning in, mock-serious)
What if the machines unionize? We’ll have to negotiate with the spin cycle.

JORDAN
Only if they promise to stop eating my left socks. I’m running out of plausible explanations.

Suddenly, the lights dim. A SPOTLIGHT appears on a lone, unclaimed sock in the center of the floor. Music swells — a dramatic, over-the-top ballad.

CASEY
(singing, arms wide)
Oh lonely sock, where is your pair?

JORDAN
(singing, off-key)
Lost in the dryer, floating in air!

They both laugh. The machines join in, humming a mechanical harmony.

CASEY
(raising a sock like a trophy)
To the unsung heroes of laundry night: may your pairs be ever found.

JORDAN
And may your lint traps never overflow.

The lights return to normal. The machines finish their cycles with a triumphant ding.

FADE OUT.
