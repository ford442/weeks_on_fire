### Scene 1 — "A Lovely Spot"

```
EXT. GARDEN TERRACE — LATE AFTERNOON

Impossibly golden light. A wrought-iron table set for two, 
half in shade. Fresh berries in a white ceramic bowl. 
Everything is immaculate. Everything is slightly wrong.

VIVIENNE VALE enters frame in monochrome — ivory blouse, 
black cigarette trousers — and surveys the table like a 
general reviewing a battlefield she has already won.

LILIANE VALE steps into the light behind her, a half-beat 
later. Midnight blue. A small leather notebook under her arm.

Both women sit simultaneously without looking at each other.

VIVIENNE
This is such a lovely spot.

LILIANE
(sampling a berry)
Perfect for a beautiful day.

VIVIENNE
And good company.

LILIANE
(beat)
These berries are so fresh.

VIVIENNE
They are a perfect pairing.

A long, appreciative silence. 
They are performing enjoyment for no visible audience.
```

### Scene 2 — "Tonight We Swap Lives"

```
EXT. GARDEN TERRACE — CONTINUOUS

Vivienne pours from a small carafe. Unhurried.

VIVIENNE
Tonight we swap lives. Starting 
with her gala.

LILIANE
(not looking up from her notebook)
Perfect. I'll attend the gala.

VIVIENNE
I'll mail her snubbery.

LILIANE
(a small, private smile)
This section is so insightful.

VIVIENNE
I agree. The examples really 
clarify it.

LILIANE
I'll make a note of that.

VIVIENNE
Good idea.

A pause. Both sip. Perfectly synchronized.

Vivienne glances toward a tree just off-frame.

VIVIENNE (CONT'D)
Were there a lot of bees in that 
tree back there?

Liliane turns and looks. Then turns back. 
Her expression does not change.

LILIANE
The wheel is on fire, darling.

VIVIENNE
Rolling down the drive.

LILIANE
Notify the next of kin, I suppose.

VIVIENNE
(considering)
Or just watch the ashes fall.

Another pause. They return to their berries. 
The light is imperceptibly redder now.
```

### Scene 3 — "Playing in the Dark"

```
INT. BALLROOM — NIGHT

Vast. Chandelier blazing. A ping-pong table runs down the 
center of the room, wildly out of place among the gilded 
furniture. 

The LIGHTS DROP to near-black. Only the chandelier remains — 
a single swaying source, casting long shadows.

Vivienne is already at one end of the table. A paddle 
appears in her hand as if it was always there.

Liliane is at the other end. She has removed her jacket. 
A dangerous sign.

They begin to play. We hear the ball before we see it.

VIVIENNE (V.O.)
Standing in this corset, breathing 
is a chore.

LILIANE (V.O.)
Playing pingpong in the dark, 
keeping up the score.

A PENDULUM swings in close-up — a grandfather clock 
against the far wall, ticking.

A SYNTHESIZER begins a low, hypnotic arpeggio.

VIVIENNE (V.O.)
Watch the pendulum swing.

LILIANE (V.O.)
Hear the synthesizer ring.

A crack in the ceiling appears. Dust falls. Neither woman 
looks up.

VIVIENNE
(still playing)
The roof is caving in.

LILIANE
(still playing)
But darling, we play to win.

The rally intensifies. The ball is a blur. The shadows dance.
```

### Scene 4 — "We Rule This Burning Town" *(Musical Sequence)*

```
EXT. VARIOUS — NIGHT

A RAPID MONTAGE of neon and fire. The city is burning —
or the city always looks like this, it's hard to tell.

Intercut with the ballroom: the rally continues.

VIVIENNE (V.O.)
Serve it up.

LILIANE (V.O.)
Strike it down.

VIVIENNE & LILIANE (V.O.)
(in unison)
We rule this burning town.

EXT. STREET — CONTINUOUS

Vivienne and Liliane walk through the city in slow motion,
neon reflections sliding across their faces. 
Behind them, something that might be a building 
is actively on fire. They do not turn around.

VIVIENNE & LILIANE (V.O.)
Racing hearts and neon light.

EXT. VELVET NIGHT — CONTINUOUS

They dance. Not with each other — each in their own orbit,
each perfectly aware of the other's position.

VIVIENNE & LILIANE (V.O.)
Dancing through the velvet night.
Back and forth, side to side.
Nowhere left for you to hide.
```

### Scene 5 — "House of Mirrors"

```
INT. HALL OF MIRRORS — CONTINUOUS

They enter through a door that wasn't there before.
An infinite hallway of mirrors, each reflecting 
a slightly different version of the two women — 
slightly wrong angles, slightly different expressions.

They walk, unhurried.

VIVIENNE
In the house of mirrors, we take 
the floor.

LILIANE
We're utterly bored—

VIVIENNE
—but give me more.

Liliane opens her leather notebook for the first time.
She writes one word. We never see what it is. 
She closes it.

VIVIENNE (CONT'D)
Matching addresses.

LILIANE
Matching the disdain.

VIVIENNE
Another perfect rally—

LILIANE
—in the pouring rain.
```

### Scene 6 — "Silver and Shadows"

```
INT./EXT. AMBIGUOUS SPACE — NIGHT

Rain, somehow, inside — or the windows are open.
Silver light on everything. 

Vivienne and Liliane continue the rally. 
The ball spins.

VIVIENNE
Silver and shadows.

She watches the ball with detached focus.

LILIANE
Spinning the ball.

She returns it without effort.

VIVIENNE & LILIANE
(quietly, together)
Watching the empire inevitably fall.

VIVIENNE
(still playing)
Don't smudge the mascara.

LILIANE
Whatever you do.

A beat.

VIVIENNE
The apocalypse is here—

LILIANE
—but we're passing through.

The game continues. A faint rumble. A structural groan.
Neither acknowledges it.

The chandelier sways.
```

### Scene 7 — "Is It Getting Warm in Here"

```
INT. BALLROOM — LATER

The crack in the ceiling has spread. Plaster dust drifts.
An orange glow bleeds through the tall windows — 
the town, genuinely burning now.

Vivienne pauses the rally. She tilts her head.
The paddle rests in her hand like a question.

VIVIENNE
Is it getting warm in here—

LILIANE
(examining the chandelier)
—or is it just the flames?

She says this the way you might say 
*"or is it just me?"*

VIVIENNE
I think it's your serve.

She bounces the ball once, twice. 
A spark drifts past the window.

VIVIENNE (CONT'D)
Try not to hit the chandelier 
this time.

LILIANE
(picking up her paddle)
Right.

A pause. They both look at the chandelier.

LILIANE (CONT'D)
Let's finish this.
```

### Scene 8 — "We Play to Win" *(Finale / Reprise)*

```
INT. BALLROOM / EXT. BURNING TOWN — INTERCUT

The final rally. The camera pulls back, wider, wider.
The ballroom and the burning street become the same space —
or we've lost track of the difference.

VIVIENNE & LILIANE
(together, at full voice — the only time)
Serve it up. Strike it down.
We rule this burning town.
Racing hearts and neon light.
Dancing through the velvet night.
Back and forth, side to side.
Nowhere left for you to hide.

The ball travels back and forth in slow motion.

VIVIENNE & LILIANE (CONT'D)
In the house of mirrors — we take 
the floor.
We're bored — but give me more fire.

The chandelier sways. The ceiling groans.
A single, tiny crack of plaster falls —
and Vivienne catches it in her palm 
like confetti.

She looks at it. A small, private smile — the most 
terrifying thing in the room.

LILIANE
(quietly)
Yeah. There more.

She serves.
```
