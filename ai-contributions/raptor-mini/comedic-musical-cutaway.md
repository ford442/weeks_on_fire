# COMEDIC MUSICAL CUTAWAY

INT. FIREWORKS STAGE - NIGHT

A single spotlight hovers above a dusty piano. Neon smoke curls through the air. We hear the muffled THUMP of a distant pinball machine.

NARRATOR (V.O.)
(whispering)
Welcome to the house where everything burns bright... and occasionally bumps into a chandelier.

CUT TO:

EXT. BURNING TOWN SQUARE - CONTINUOUS

A herd of PAPER DOLLS march in slo-mo, each wearing a different costume: a corset, a tuxedo jacket, a glittered referee shirt.

CHARACTER 1
(squinting at a scoreboard made of mirrors)
Is this the feeling of fire, or did somebody crank the synth too loud?

CHARACTER 2
(smiling, holding a ping-pong paddle)
It’s both. We’re playing pinball with the apocalypse, and the high score is “more sparkles.”

MUSIC BEGINS: a quirky, pulsing rhythm with a soft xylophone and a booming bass.

ACTION: A FLAMING BALL ricochets off a chandelier, sparks falling like confetti. Two dancers appear, their moves a mix of ballroom and video-game glitches.

CHARACTER 1
(tossing a berry into the air)
If the roof caves in, can we still call it a stage?

CHARACTER 2
(grinning)
Only if we keep dancing through the ashes. Watch—

They spin. The walls collapse into a giant HOUSE OF MIRRORS, each reflection showing a slightly different version of the same argument.

CHARACTER 1
(pointing to a mirror)
You’re being dramatic.

CHARACTER 2
(looking back at themselves)
No, I’m being theatrical. There’s a difference.

PARENTHESES: the camera PULLS BACK to reveal a metaphorical PING-PONG TABLE built from melody lines.

CHARACTER 1
(whispering)
Try not to hit the chandelier this time.

CHARACTER 2
(raising their paddle)
That’s half the fun.

MUSIC SWELLS. The scene becomes a flash of neon ink: a disco ball, a circus tent, a burning town all mashed together. The characters trade lines like a duet.

CHARACTER 1
(half-song, half-speech)
We’re bored of quiet mornings and polite fires.

CHARACTER 2
(half-song, half-speech)
So serve it up, strike it down, make this burning town our playground.

CUT TO:

INT. LIVING ROOM / DREAM SEQUENCE - LATER

A couch appears, draped in sequins. The two characters collapse onto it, laughing through smoke.

CHARACTER 1
(sighing)
If this is the end, make it loud.

CHARACTER 2
(kissing a sparkler)
If this is the end, at least there’s a soundtrack.

NARRATOR (V.O.)
Sometimes the funniest stories are the ones that burn slow and leave you humming on the way out.

FADE OUT.
