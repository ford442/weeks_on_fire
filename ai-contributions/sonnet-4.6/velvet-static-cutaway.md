# "VELVET STATIC" — A Musical Cutaway Scene

*An interlude for two people who should not be talking to each other, at the worst possible moment, in the most beautiful possible light.*

---

## INT. ROOFTOP GARDEN — BURNING CITY — NIGHT

*Smoke rises from the streets below. Up here: fairy lights strung between dead trees, a single garden table with two mismatched chairs, a portable speaker playing something that should be sad but isn't. The city below is mid-catastrophe. Up here, the catastrophe is ambient.*

*VERA, late 30s, impeccable posture, wine in hand, watches the fires with the focused attention of someone who planned for this.*

*REMY, same era, jacket from a different decade, arrives through the rooftop door carrying two more glasses and a bottle of something too expensive for the occasion.*

---

**REMY**  
*(looking at the skyline)*  
You called it.

**VERA**  
I call everything. I just don't usually stay to watch.

**REMY**  
And tonight?

**VERA**  
*(a beat)*  
Better view up here.

*He sits. She doesn't look at him. He pours anyway.*

**REMY**  
The Hammond building went down about twenty minutes ago.

**VERA**  
I saw.

**REMY**  
You didn't seem surprised.

**VERA**  
I was never surprised by the Hammond building.

*Beat.*

**REMY**  
Is that a metaphor?

**VERA**  
*(finally turning)*  
Everything is a metaphor. That's the problem with an arts education.

---

## MUSICAL CUTAWAY — "VELVET STATIC"

*The portable speaker cuts out. A moment of silence. Then from somewhere across the rooftops: music. A synthesizer, searching. Then finding.*

### VERSE ONE — VERA *(alone, almost to herself)*

*There are things I kept in envelopes,*  
*Things I meant to send someday,*  
*Now the post office is on fire, darling,*  
*And I've run out of things to say.*

*I practiced all the clever exits.*  
*Packed them neatly, labeled, stored.*  
*But you showed up with better wine than I expected*  
*And I just... couldn't be that bored.*

### VERSE TWO — REMY *(standing now, looking at the city)*

*I had a plan. It was a good plan.*  
*Sensible shoes. A cab at ten.*  
*But the cab company's apparently on fire*  
*So here I am with you again.*

*I keep arriving at your rooftop.*  
*Like a compass finding north.*  
*You say it's gravity. I say it's something stupider.*  
*We both know it's worth.*

### CHORUS — BOTH

*In the velvet static*  
*Between the stations we can't hold,*  
*In the version of this evening*  
*That we'll never quite be told—*

*We are not in love.*  
*We are not not in love.*  
*We are standing on a rooftop*  
*Watching everything go up,*  
*And somehow that is something.*  
*Somehow that is enough.*

---

## INT. ROOFTOP GARDEN — CONTINUOUS

*The music fades back to street noise. Distant sirens. A glass clinking.*

**VERA**  
You know what I've never understood?

**REMY**  
Name one thing.

**VERA**  
Why catastrophe always sounds like a 1997 trance record.

**REMY**  
*(listening)*  
... That's not wrong.

**VERA**  
Four-on-the-floor kick. Sweeping pad. Some woman singing something you can almost understand.

**REMY**  
Maybe that's what the end of the world wants to be.

**VERA**  
What?

**REMY**  
Memorable. Danceable. Slightly too loud for the setting.

*She considers this. Looks back at the fires.*

**VERA**  
I hate it when you're right.

**REMY**  
I know. You've hated it for eleven years.

**VERA**  
*(under her breath)*  
Eleven and a half.

---

## BRIDGE — VERA *(sung, not quite to him)*

*I made a list of all the reasons*  
*This was not a good idea.*  
*I laminated it. Framed it.*  
*Kept it somewhere you'd see it.*

*You kept arriving anyway.*  
*Wine in hand. Wrong jacket.*  
*Saying absolutely nothing useful*  
*And somehow meaning all of it.*

---

## INT. ROOFTOP GARDEN — LATER

*The fires have settled into a steady glow. The city is not ending — it is just very committed to the aesthetic.*

**REMY**  
We should probably—

**VERA**  
Probably.

*Neither moves.*

**REMY**  
The stairs are still clear.

**VERA**  
Are they.

**REMY**  
I checked on the way up.

**VERA**  
*(a small smile)*  
You checked the exits.

**REMY**  
Force of habit. Eleven and a half years of you.

*Beat.*

**VERA**  
Stay until the lights change.

**REMY**  
What lights?

**VERA**  
*(gesturing at the burning horizon)*  
Those. They go amber, then gold. About four in the morning. For about six minutes, the whole city looks like it meant to do this.

**REMY**  
Like it planned it.

**VERA**  
Like it planned it.

*He settles back into the chair.*

**REMY**  
*(quiet)*  
You've watched this before.

**VERA**  
Not like this.

---

## FINAL CHORUS — BOTH *(barely above speaking)*

*In the velvet static*  
*We find the frequency we share,*  
*Not love exactly,*  
*But something that lives there—*

*Something that stays*  
*When the music drops away,*  
*When the fires go amber and gold*  
*And we run out of things to say.*

*We are standing on a rooftop.*  
*It is almost four a.m.*  
*The city is still beautiful.*  
*So are you.*  
*So.*  
*Are.*  
*You.*

*Blackout.*

*The music doesn't stop — it fades into static, then into silence, then into something that might be birdsong.*

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Musical Style:** Slow-build ambient trance with 90s rave DNA — begins minimal (single synth, no kick) and expands into euphoric four-on-the-floor for the chorus, then strips back to ambient for the final moments. Think late-night radio bleed between two stations.

**Visual Direction for Grok Imagine:**
- Rooftop garden lit by fairy lights and distant orange glow
- Two figures: one standing, one seated — never quite facing each other until the final chorus
- City below in deep amber/smoke haze — beautiful and doomed
- Mirror motif: the speaker's grill, the wine glasses, puddles on the rooftop catching the fire-light
- Final shot: wide, pulling back to reveal the city skyline with two tiny figures and one portable speaker

**Tone:** Romantic comedy pretending to be a tragedy, or vice versa. The apocalypse is furniture, not subject. The subject is two people who have been circling each other for eleven and a half years and are running out of reasons not to stop circling.

**Runtime:** ~3:10 (can trim bridge if needed for tighter cut)

**Key Line:** *"We are not in love. We are not not in love."* — the thesis of the whole scene, and possibly the series.
