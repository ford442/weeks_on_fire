# "LAST CALL AT THE COMET LOUNGE" — A Karaoke Cutaway

*A musical interlude for a karaoke bar at the end of the world, an owner who has heard every song twice, and a man who has finally come to sing the right one.*

---

## INT. THE COMET LOUNGE — KARAOKE BAR — 11:47 PM

*A small, low-ceilinged karaoke bar on what is clearly the last night of operation. The good news: there are still strings of lights. The bad news: half of them have given up. Outside, through the smoke-glass window, a comet is in the wrong half of the sky. It has been there for three weeks. Nobody references it anymore. The booze, however, has gotten more expensive.*

*MARGOT, the owner, ageless in the way that bar owners are ageless, polishes a glass with a cloth that has seen every decade of this place. The karaoke screen behind her is paused on a track that no one is singing. A blinking cursor where a name should be.*

*The door chimes. NED enters. Wet hair. He has either come in from rain that isn't falling or has done something to himself in a bathroom across town. He carries a small piece of paper, folded into a square so many times it's nearly the size of a postage stamp.*

---

**MARGOT**  
*(not looking up)*  
We're closing.

**NED**  
The sign says open till one.

**MARGOT**  
The sign is from 2009. The sign and I have an arrangement.

*She finally looks up. Recognizes him. Doesn't react.*

**MARGOT (CONT'D)**  
Oh. It's you.

**NED**  
It's me.

**MARGOT**  
You're late.

**NED**  
I'm forty years late, Margot.

**MARGOT**  
*(setting down the glass)*  
Then a few more minutes won't hurt. Sit down. The booth's still yours.

*He sits in a booth that has clearly been waiting. The vinyl is split. The table has a ring on it from a glass that was set down in 1984 and never picked up.*

---

**MARGOT**  
What are you drinking?

**NED**  
What did I used to drink?

**MARGOT**  
Something you couldn't afford and didn't like.

**NED**  
Make it that.

*She pours something amber. Brings it over. Doesn't sit down. Doesn't leave either.*

**MARGOT**  
You here for a song?

**NED**  
*(unfolding the paper, slowly)*  
I'm here for *the* song.

**MARGOT**  
*(a long beat)*  
... Of course you are.

---

## MUSICAL CUTAWAY — "LAST CALL"

*MARGOT walks behind the karaoke machine. She doesn't ask what song. She knows. She has known for forty years what song was queued up behind that folded paper. She types nothing. She presses a single button. The screen wakes up. The opening synth — a slow, late-night, half-asleep arpeggio — begins.*

*NED stands. Walks to the small wooden stage. Takes the microphone with the cracked grip. The lyrics begin to scroll.*

---

### VERSE ONE — NED

*The night you closed early on a Tuesday,*  
*I had the song. I had the nerve almost.*  
*I had the quarter in my pocket for the jukebox*  
*And the wrong words in my throat to make a toast.*

*You said "next week" and I said "next week,"*  
*And forty Tuesdays turned to forty years.*  
*The jukebox broke. The quarter found a coat pocket.*  
*I learned to live without and call it cheer.*

---

### INTERLUDE — MARGOT *(from behind the bar, not into a microphone)*

*The bar was never empty after closing.*  
*Just emptied of the one face I'd been hoping in.*  
*I stocked the same brand of bourbon for forty winters*  
*And told myself I just liked the label.*

*The comet came. The neighborhood went strange.*  
*I kept the lights on out of stubborn habit.*  
*Tonight I almost locked the door at eleven*  
*And then I heard a stranger turn the handle.*

---

### CHORUS — BOTH *(NED into the mic, MARGOT into a glass)*

*Last call, last call,*  
*The town is on the wrong side of the sky.*  
*Last call, last call,*  
*And we are out of reasons to be shy.*

*The comet hasn't moved in twenty-one days.*  
*The streetlights are on a generator and a prayer.*  
*If the world has decided to wait for the chorus*  
*Then darling, we should probably sing it like it's there.*

---

## INT. THE COMET LOUNGE — CONTINUOUS

*The song continues, but the music drops to under-dialogue volume. He sits back down with the microphone in his hand. She comes around the bar with the bottle.*

**MARGOT**  
You sing better than you used to.

**NED**  
I've had time to practice.

**MARGOT**  
In your car?

**NED**  
In my car. In the shower. At the funerals of everyone who knew this song.

*Beat.*

**MARGOT**  
That's a long set list.

**NED**  
*(quiet)*  
It got shorter every year.

*She pours herself one. Sits across from him. The booth fits her like it has always fit her, because it has.*

**MARGOT**  
Why tonight?

**NED**  
*(gesturing at the window, the comet)*  
The sky cleared its throat.

**MARGOT**  
That's not why.

**NED**  
No.

**MARGOT**  
Then why?

**NED**  
*(folding the paper back up, smaller and smaller)*  
Because I was finally more afraid of dying with the song in my pocket than of singing it badly.

*She nods. Drinks. Looks at him for a long time.*

**MARGOT**  
You didn't sing it badly.

**NED**  
I know.

**MARGOT**  
That's worse, isn't it.

**NED**  
*(small laugh)*  
Yeah. A little.

---

## BRIDGE — MARGOT *(now standing, walking toward the stage)*

*There is a version of this evening*  
*That happened in 1984.*  
*In that version you came back in on the Tuesday.*  
*In that version we walked out the side door.*

*We didn't. We chose the other one.*  
*The one where I kept the bar and you kept the song.*  
*The one where the comet eventually shows up*  
*And tells us politely we got the timing wrong.*

*But here is the funny thing about timing:*  
*The song doesn't actually care.*  
*It will sit in a pocket for forty Tuesdays*  
*And still know its own chorus when it's there.*

---

## INT. THE COMET LOUNGE — CONTINUOUS

*She takes the microphone from him. Steps up onto the stage — the first time in twenty years, by her count. The screen scrolls to the final chorus.*

---

### FINAL CHORUS — MARGOT, then BOTH

**MARGOT** *(alone, fragile, exact)*

*Last call, last call,*  
*The town is on the wrong side of the sky.*  
*Last call, last call,*  
*And we are out of reasons to be shy.*

**BOTH** *(she pulls him up beside her — he comes, no resistance left)*

*We were always going to end up here.*  
*In a booth, in a bar, in the year of the comet.*  
*We were always going to mistime the moment*  
*And then make a moment of the mistiming.*

*Last call, last call,*  
*Pour one out for the people we were.*  
*Last call, last call,*  
*And one for the people we still might be.*

*The world is ending in a beautiful key.*  
*The world is ending in a beautiful key.*  
*The world is ending,*  
*And darling,*  
*Finally,*  
*Finally,*  
*We.*

---

## INT. THE COMET LOUNGE — CONTINUOUS

*The song ends on a held note, then a single click as the karaoke machine cycles back to its idle screen — a blinking cursor where a name should be. They stand on the stage. Microphone between them. Neither of them moves to put it down.*

*Outside, the comet does something subtle with its tail. Nobody sees it. The two of them are looking at each other.*

**NED**  
Are you closing?

**MARGOT**  
*(without looking at the clock)*  
Not anymore.

*She steps down from the stage. Walks to the front door. Turns the sign — slowly, deliberately — from OPEN to OPEN.*

*Blackout.*

*Over the black: the sound of two glasses being set down on a table at the same time. The hum of an old karaoke machine. Far away, very faint, the sound of someone else opening a door.*

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Musical Style:** Late-night lounge ballad with 90s synth-pad underpinning. Slow tempo (≈68 BPM). Sparse instrumentation — Rhodes piano, a single warm pad, brushed snare. The chorus opens into a wider production but never goes "big" — this is karaoke, not a stadium. The female voice (Margot) should sound like it has been waiting decades to be in tune. The male voice (Ned) should sound like he has been singing alone in a car.

**Visual Direction for Grok Imagine:**
- Tight, warm interior — amber bulbs, smoke-glass, vinyl booths
- Karaoke screen with a blinking cursor as a recurring motif (the cursor = the unsaid)
- The comet visible only through the window — never the focus, always the weather
- The folded paper as a physical anchor: smaller and smaller through the scene
- Final shot: the sign turning from OPEN to OPEN — same word, different meaning

**Tone:** A duet that is also a confession that is also a closing-time joke. The apocalypse is local color, not subject. The subject is the song that was in the pocket the whole time. Comic where it can be (the sign from 2009, the bourbon she stocked for forty winters), tender where it has to be (the funerals of everyone who knew the song).

**Runtime:** ~3:40 (the bridge can be cut to 3:00 if needed for an episode)

**Series Connections:**
- The comet motif could echo across episodes — same comet, different rooftops
- "We were always going to mistime the moment / and then make a moment of the mistiming" — possible recurring lyric across the series, sung by different characters in different scenes
- The blinking cursor on the karaoke screen pairs visually with the mirror motifs from Episode 1

**Casting Notes:**
- MARGOT should be the kind of presence that makes silence feel like a held note
- NED should look like a man who has been carrying a piece of paper for forty years — which is to say, he should look like he never quite unpacked
