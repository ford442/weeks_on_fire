"THE ALL-NITE GRIDDLE HAS SEEN BETTER HOURS" -- A Musical Cutaway Scene

A scene for a waitress who refuses to acknowledge the end of her shift and the only
man in the city who still carries exact change.


INT. THE ALL-NITE GRIDDLE -- DINER -- 3:47 AM (FOURTH NIGHT OF THE DIMMING)

The fluorescent tubes overhead pulse in a rhythm that would be annoying if anyone
still expected them to stop. Three of the eight are dead. Two flicker with the
dedication of understudies who know they'll never go on. The remaining three hum
at half-resolve, casting everything in a color best described as "optimistic
jaundice."

The windows face east. They should show sunrise in forty-three minutes. They have
not shown sunrise in ninety-six hours. The sky outside is the color of a bruise
that is considering becoming something worse. Nobody mentions it. The locals have
an unspoken agreement: if the sky is going to misbehave, they are not going to be
the ones to make it self-conscious.

DOLORES, 40s, hair that started as a bun sometime last Tuesday and has since
become an architecture, refills a coffee cup that does not need refilling. The
cup belongs to:

ALDEN, 50s, a man who has been wearing the same corduroy jacket through three
seasons because it has finally achieved the right level of defeat. He sits at the
counter's fourth stool -- not the first (too desperate), not the last (too
narrative), but the fourth, which is the stool of a man who has thought about
this. He is sorting nickels into stacks of five. There are seven stacks. He has
thirty-eight cents more than he needs for the meal he hasn't ordered yet.

DOLORES
(pouring)
You're going to die of not sleeping.

ALDEN
(counting)
I'm going to die of something. Might as well be thematic.

DOLORES
Thematic?

ALDEN
You've got the diner open at four in the morning with no eggs, no cook, and a
sky that looks like a bad suggestion. I'm sitting here counting nickels into
lighthouses. Something's going to be thematic or nothing is.

DOLORES
(setting the pot down)
We have eggs.

ALDEN
You have eggs that are thinking about being a different concept. I ordered
scrambled an hour ago. They're still voting on it.

DOLORES
(smiling, almost)
The stove's on a timer. It heats for twelve minutes, rests for twelve. It's
got a rhythm.

ALDEN
Everything's got a rhythm tonight. The lights. The stove. My indigestion.
The damn sky, whatever it's doing out there.

DOLORES
(looking out the window, not at it)
The sky is doing what skies do.

ALDEN
Skies go light. Then dark. Then light. This one's stuck on a mood.

DOLORES
Maybe it's thinking.

ALDEN
That's what I'm afraid of. A sky with an interior life. Next it'll want
coffee.

DOLORES
It can get in line.

She tops off his cup. He slides one of his nickel lighthouses toward her. She
doesn't take it, but she notices.

ALDEN
You ever close this place?

DOLORES
It's called the All-Nite Griddle.

ALDEN
That's a promise, not a contract.

DOLORES
I made it to my mother when I was twenty-two. Told her I'd run the night
shift until she got better. She didn't. I stayed. Now it's what I do.

ALDEN
How long ago?

DOLORES
Nineteen years, four months, and -- she looks at the clock -- eleven days.

ALDEN
You're not still doing it for her.

DOLORES
I'm doing it for the person I was when I made the promise. That kid believed
in things. I like keeping her company.

A long beat. The fluorescents pulse. Somewhere, a jukebox turns itself on
without being touched. Track one. The song is old, slow, and sounds like it
was recorded in a room where someone had just stopped crying.

ALDEN
I was here the night they announced the Dimming. You remember? The
president came on the little TV in the corner. Said the sun would be
"temporarily unavailable." Like it was a website.

DOLORES
I remember. You ordered pie.

ALDEN
You remember what kind?

DOLORES
Cherry. You said if the world was going to end, it should at least be
allegorical.

ALDEN
(stunned she remembers)
That was eleven weeks ago.

DOLORES
Eleven weeks, three days. You come in every night. You order something you
don't eat. You stack coins into little buildings. You're the most reliable
thing in my life right now, and I don't even know your name.

ALDEN
Alden.

DOLORES
Dolores. Though if you've been coming here eleven weeks, you knew that.

ALDEN
I knew it. I wanted you to say it.

DOLORES
(softly)
Why?

ALDEN
Because you're the only person in this city who's still doing what they said
they'd do. I wanted to hear the name of someone who keeps promises.

The jukebox clicks. The song changes. Something new. Something that sounds
like it was written for this moment by someone who was watching from the next
booth.

MUSICAL CUTAWAY -- "BURN THE BREAD"

The diner shifts. The lights don't change, but the shadows agree to behave
differently. Alden stands -- not dramatically, just the way a man stands when
he's been sitting too long and his body has decided to try something new.
Dolores doesn't move from behind the counter, but her posture shifts from
"waitress on duty" to "woman in a room where something might happen."

The sound swells: a warm, worn Wurlitzer organ, a brush-on-snare rhythm like
someone keeping time on a diner counter with a spoon, and a single electric
guitar that sounds like it learned to sing by listening to people say goodbye
in bus stations.

VERSE ONE -- ALDEN
(standing by the counter, not quite singing to her, not quite not)

  I had a house with a mortgage and a calendar,
  A wife who left for all the right reasons,
  A job that kept me busy dying slower,
  And a talent for mistaking all four seasons.
  Then the sun got shy and the dark moved in,
  And I found this seat where the coffee's thin,
  And a waitress who won't close up or give in,
  So I guess I'm staying till she wins.

VERSE TWO -- DOLORES
(wiping a counter that is already clean, not looking at him)

  I had a mother who believed in mornings,
  A husband who believed in Santa Fe,
  A diner with a neon sign that's burning,
  And a promise that I never learned to break.
  Then the eggs got strange and the regulars left,
  And the sky turned the color of regret,
  And this man who builds his money into theft --
  no, into towers -- I forget the rest.

ALDEN
(overlapping, smiling)

  Lighthouses. They're lighthouses.

DOLORES
(finally looking at him)

  Lighthouses. Right. Of course they are.
  For all the ships that pass through here.
  The fleet of me, the navy of my missing years,
  Sailing through this All-Nite harbor.

CHORUS -- BOTH
(Alden has moved to the other side of the counter. They're not touching.
They're not not touching. The space between them is exactly the width of a
promise kept too long.)

  Burn the bread if the oven's still on,
  Sing the song if you know the words,
  I've been waiting for the sun to come back,
  But I'll settle for someone who stays till it does.
  Burn the bread, let the smoke rise,
  It's the only prayer the sky deserves,
  I've been talking to the dark for eleven weeks,
  And the dark says you're the first one
  Who ever talked back like it mattered.

BRIDGE -- ALDEN
(picking up a napkin, folding it into something that might be a bird
or might be an apology)

  I don't know what I'm doing here, Dolores.
  I don't know what anyone's doing anywhere.
  But I know you refill a cup that no one ordered,
  And you remember my pie was cherry,
  And that feels like the kind of knowledge
  That used to pass between people
  Before we all got so good at forgetting.

BRIDGE -- DOLORES
(taking the napkin, unfolding it, folding it again into something
that might be a heart or might be a map to somewhere the sun still rises)

  I don't know why the sky won't lighten.
  I don't know why I keep the griddle warm.
  But I know your stacks of five mean something,
  Even if you can't say what,
  And I know that tonight feels different
  From the ninety-six nights before it,
  And I'm scared that different might mean hope,
  And I'm scared that hope might mean I'm still alive enough to want things.

CHORUS -- BOTH
(They are closer now. The counter is still between them. Some things
are geography, not distance.)

  Burn the bread if the oven's still on,
  Sing the song if you know the words,
  I've been waiting for the sun to come back,
  But I'll settle for someone who stays till it does.
  Burn the bread, let the smoke rise,
  It's the only prayer the sky deserves,
  I've been talking to the dark for eleven weeks,
  And the dark says you're the first one
  Who ever talked back like it mattered.

OUTRO -- DOLORES
(alone, almost to herself, as the music starts to fade)

  Burn the bread. Burn the bread. Burn the bread.
  Someone's still here to smell it burning.
  That has to mean something.
  That has to mean enough.

The music dissolves back into the hum of the fluorescents. The flickering
continues. The sky outside remains unchanged. But something in the diner has
shifted -- the air, maybe, or the angle of the not-light, or the fact that
two people are looking at each other with something that might, if you were
feeling generous, be called recognition.


INT. THE ALL-NITE GRIDDLE -- CONTINUOUS

ALDEN sits back down on the fourth stool. DOLORES picks up the coffee pot.
The routine resumes, but the rhythm is different now.

ALDEN
You want to know something stupid?

DOLORES
I feel like I'm going to hear it either way.

ALDEN
I keep coming here because this is the only place where the dark makes
sense. Everywhere else, people are pretending it's temporary. "The sun'll
be back." "They're working on it." "Just a phase." You're the only person
who isn't lying about it.

DOLORES
What am I saying that's so honest?

ALDEN
You're keeping the lights on. That's an opinion. You're not saying it'll
get better. You're just saying... here. Still here.

DOLORES
(quietly)
Still here.

ALDEN
Still here is the bravest thing anyone's said to me in months.

DOLORES
Then let me say something braver.

ALDEN
I'm listening.

DOLORES
The eggs are actually ready. Have been for ten minutes. I was waiting to
see if you'd still be here when they were.

ALDEN
And?

DOLORES
You're still here.

She turns and walks to the kitchen. We hear a plate being prepared. The
sound of a spatula, a sizzle, something simple and human in a world that
has forgotten how to be either.

ALDEN
(calling after her)
Dolores?

DOLORES (O.S.)
Yeah?

ALDEN
If the sun comes back -- when the sun comes back --

DOLORES (O.S.)
(interrupting)
I'll still be here. The All-Nite Griddle doesn't close. That's a promise,
not a name.

She returns with two plates. Sets one in front of him. Keeps one for
herself. She comes around the counter and sits on the fifth stool -- the
one right next to his.

ALDEN
Since when do waitresses eat with the customers?

DOLORES
Since the customers started being the only other person in the room.

They eat. The fluorescents pulse. The sky stays dark. The jukebox plays
something old and soft and not quite audible.

But for the first time in ninety-six hours, two people are eating breakfast
together while the world waits for morning.

And somewhere, in the distance, a sound that might be the first edge of
light. Or might just be the oven, still warm. Or might be nothing at all.

But they hear it.

FADE OUT.

SCENE NOTES FOR PRODUCTION

Setting: A single-location diner set. Practical fluorescent lighting with
       flicker-controlled dimmers. The "Dimming" effect can be achieved
       with a static backdrop or rear-projection -- no expensive VFX needed.

Music Style: "Burn the Bread" should be arranged for Wurlitzer organ,
       brushed drums, and a single clean electric guitar (Telecaster or
       similar). Think: late-period Tom Waits meets Sharon Van Etten in
       a 3 AM conversation. Tempo: slow, 68 BPM. Key: E major with
       modal borrowings in the bridge.

Performance Notes: The song should feel discovered, not performed. Alden
       and Dolores aren't singers -- they're people who find themselves
       in a song. Slightly off-mic, slightly unsure, completely committed.

Minimax Music Prompt Reference: See kimi_minimax_guide.md for vocal
       styling guidance. Suggest: "worn warm baritone" (Alden) and
       "hushed contralto with frayed edges" (Dolores).

Grok Imagine Visual Prompts:
  - "Diner interior with flickering fluorescent lights, warm shadows,
     two people at a counter, cinematic 2.35:1, film grain, amber tones"
  - "Stack of nickels arranged into a small lighthouse shape, macro
     photography, shallow depth of field, diner counter surface"
  - "Pre-dawn sky in unnatural deep bruised purple, viewed through
     diner window, condensation on glass, faint neon reflection"

Estimated Runtime: 6-8 minutes (dialogue + musical cutaway)
Suggested Episode Placement: Episode 2 or 3 -- works as a standalone
       emotional beat or as a connective tissue scene between larger
       narrative moments.
